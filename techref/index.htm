  • No one has it all figured out. We just build models that seem to fit what we've seen, and use them to predict what will happen, and then change what we do, based on how we think that will help us control the future.
  • All models are wrong, some models are useful. Simple models are quick and often accurate enough. Pretending that models must be perfect; all or nothing thinking in general, is the single most common and most damaging mental illness.
  • Good models can be improved by finding reality that they don't predict, and updating them to work better.
  • Models which can't be disproven or which don't predict the future aren't very useful. It's comforting to have beliefs or models based on limited data, or unprovable ideas, but we must never let those become dogma, or stand in the way of more usefl models.
  • It's easy to confuse failure with the good work of disproving and improving models, especially when we get attached to a particulear view of the world.
  • Rapid, iterative, "failure" is the best way to advance good models and avoid FAILURE. "Fail, First, Fast, Fix."
  • Models work best when combined between multiple disciplines, irrespective of how new or old they are, in a collaborative environment. Analog signals. Mechanical computing. Sensor fusion. Ancient youth. Modern classics.
